Introduction

Introduction to this Document

This confirmation document outlines my practice-based doctoral research, which investigates how virtual ecological art practices might turn toward nature through the intertwined philosophies of Taoism and Deleuze and Guattari in a posthuman era. I am undertaking this research because of a long-standing fascination with mushrooms, my embodied encounters with fungi and immersive art, and a desire to rethink human–technology–nature relations beyond anthropocentric control.

Methodologically, I approach the research as a cultivation process that combines philosophical inquiry, generative and immersive digital art, somatic practices informed by Daoist cosmology, and post-qualitative, relational modes of data generation. The work is supported by practice communities in art, design, and somatic movement who participate in exhibitions and workshops. The research unfolds over the duration of my doctoral study, with key phases marked by exhibitions, iterative workshops, and a planned programme of practice and reflection through to completion.

The document is structured to guide the reader through this trajectory: it opens by positioning my practice as the primary point of departure, before situating my own encounter and positionality, then establishing the methodological metaphor of the “morning mushroom” from Zhuangzi and its philosophical subsoil. From there, the writing elaborates the broader theoretical environment, outlines the Morning Mushroom Method, presents preliminary exhibitions and workshops as sites of practice, details ethical considerations, and finally maps the planned route to completion.
