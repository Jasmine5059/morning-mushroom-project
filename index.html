Lijuan(Jasmine) Kang

Morning mushroom pathway

I am fascinated by mushrooms.

I often wonder why they look the way they do,

with a kind of innocent strangeness.

Their scent is damp, their taste vivid,

appearing at random,

deep in the forest, among rotting leaves,

at the moment, I lower my gaze.

When I was a child, I often followed my grandfather into the woods.

We walked slowly, heads down,

learning to recognise by shapes, colours, and smells,

learning to trust experience, and to practise caution.

Mushrooms felt like gifts that allowed themselves to be found.

In the rainforests of Southeast Asia,

mushrooms opened for me an almost fairy-tale dimension,

I encountered them again in painting,

in Van Gogh’s turbulent wheat fields,

in spaces where colour spills and dimensions slip.
